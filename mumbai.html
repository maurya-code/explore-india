MUMBAI

Overview

Mumbai (formerly known as Bombay) is the city of dreams and the land of opportunities. The capital of Maharashtra, Mumbai is also a vital sea port. It is the financial and industrial capital of India and one of the most densely populated cities in the world.

Tt’s also the numerous tourist attractions that draw in thousands of visitors from around the world. The most popular of these are the Elephanta Caves and the Gateway of India. There are also gardens, theme parks, and beautiful beaches. Those who like a good bargain can head to Fashion Street or Chor Bazaar for the latest in clothing and accessories. Though Mumbai is famous for its street foods (especially the Vada Pav), it also offers various dining options from quaint coffee shops to award-winning restaurants. Home to people from various cultures, Mumbai is a true representation of the phrase ‘unity in diversity.


POPULAR PLACES

GATEWAY OF INDIA

This Mumbai landmark was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Bombay in the year 1911. Ironically, the construction of the structure only completed in 1924 and it has, ever since, been one of the most visited tourist attractions in Mumbai.Not only will a visitor get some terrific photography shots at this place, but you will also find local vendors selling lip-smacking street foods like bhelpuri, Sev puri, etc.

HAJI ALI DARGAH

About Haji Ali Dargah Famous for being the mosque in the middle of the Arabian Sea, Haji Ali is situated 500 yards from the shoreline of Mumbai. Haji Ali is a paramount religious site of Mumbai that houses the tomb of Saint Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari. The 400-year-old mosque is marvelled at for its intricate Indo-Islamic architecture and the unusual location. It is believed that the Haji Ali Dargah was built by a wealthy merchant known as Haji Ali Shah Bukhari.

SIDDHIVINAYAK TEMPLE

One of the richer temples in Mumbai, Siddhivinayak Temple, is located at Prabhadevi and dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Also popular as Navsacha Ganpati locally, the temple was built in 1801, but gained prominence only after 1952. Brimming with pilgrims on an ordinary day, you should spare at least an hour or two when visiting this spot because of the long queues of devotees waiting for their turn to visit the temple.

ELEPHANTA CAVES

Your trip to Elephanta Caves is going to be etched in your memory forever. It starts from the very moment you set your foot on the ferry that will take you to the Elephanta Island- an hour's trip from the central city. As you reach the ancient caves created between AD 450 and 750, it may feel you've stepped into a different world, almost surreal.

October to June is the ideal time to visit Maharashtra as during these months the state enjoys a cool and invigorating weather.

Best Season to Visit

From October to March, the weather loses its stinging heat and becomes bearable with temperatures ranging between 18°C to 32°C. The months between November and January see many festivals and fairs organised such as Diwali festival in November, Christmas in December and in January, the three-day annual Visakha Utsav festival is held highlighting Andhra culture.

Must try

2. Bandstand in Bandra is a long rocky walkaway by the sea. Known as one of the famous hangout spots and jogger’s park, Bandstand has gained its popularity as a romantic hotspot nowadays.

How to reach


BY FLIGHT

Mumbai, the capital city has two airports, one international (Sahara International Airport) and Santa Cruz domestic airport. All leading domestic and international airlines operate regular flights to and from Mumbai. Other major domestic airports in Maharashtra are located at Pune, Aurangabad, Nagpur, Kolhapur and, Sholapur.

BY RAIL

Mumbai is the major railway junction of Maharashtra, which is served by many important trains that link the city to other cities of India. There are also major Railway Stations in or close by the tourist destinations.

BY ROAD

Maharashtra has an excellent road network. A number of national highways and state highways connect all major cities of Maharashtra to other parts of the country.
